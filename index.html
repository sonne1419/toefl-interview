Stage 0 — Learn from Samples
Samples
📖 How to use this page ▼
Every sample below comes with a strong answer to the same question. You choose how closely to follow it. Pick whichever way suits you today — you can use a different one on the next question.
Copy — Read the sample until you can hold it in your head, tick Hide sample above the Sample Answer column, and say it back in your own voice.
Translate — Use the language switch above the table to show the sample in your own language. Read the meaning, then say it back in English without looking at the English text.
Adapt — Pick a reason above the “Your Idea” column and press Help me with ideas. Keep the sample's shape, but speak about the new idea instead.
About the “Your Idea” column
This column shows a short idea, written as a few keywords joined by arrows, like: shop online → less stress → calm weekends. It is not a script. Each arrow is one step that you expand into a full spoken sentence, so a short line becomes several sentences of speech. The Sample Answer column never changes; your idea sits beside it as a different idea you can build the same shape around.
Create — Answer the question with your own content. Use the sample only as a reminder of how a full answer is built.
You have 45 seconds. Scoring starts on its own as soon as you stop — there is no button to press. You will get a band score with feedback, and when the band is below 5, a comparison showing what a strong answer does differently.
💡 The sample stays visible while you record unless you tick Hide sample first. You can untick it anytime.
💡 The comparison always measures you against the sample, even when you wrote your own content. Treat it as a picture of what a top answer contains, not a list of things you got wrong.
💡 Recording again replaces your previous attempt at this question.
